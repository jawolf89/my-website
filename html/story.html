Gungrave follows Brandon Heat and Harry MacDowell, two friends growing up in a gritty city where survival often means joining the criminal underworld. After a tragic incident forces them into hiding, they are recruited by Millennion, a powerful crime syndicate. Under the leadership of “Big Daddy,” Brandon becomes a loyal enforcer, while Harry’s ambition drives him to climb the organization’s ranks. The two share a brotherly bond, but their goals begin to drift apart.

As the years pass, Brandon earns a reputation as Millennion’s most skilled sweeper, dedicated to protecting the syndicate’s honor and Big Daddy’s vision. Harry, however, grows restless and impatient, believing the only way forward is to take control himself. Their friendship begins to strain under Harry’s ruthless decisions, as his ambition starts to threaten the stability of everything they built together.

Tensions erupt when Harry betrays Big Daddy in a violent coup to seize power. Brandon, torn between his loyalty to his friend and to Millennion, confronts Harry but is ultimately killed in the confrontation. Harry, believing Brandon to be dead forever, consolidates his rule, reshaping Millennion into a harsher, more self-serving empire. Brandon’s death marks a turning point for the organization — and for Harry’s soul.

Years later, in a world where advanced technology allows for the creation of undead super-soldiers known as Necrolyze, Brandon is resurrected as “Beyond the Grave.” Stripped of most of his humanity, Grave’s only goal is revenge against Harry and the syndicate that destroyed the ideals they once fought for. Alongside Mika, the daughter of Big Daddy, Grave battles through waves of deadly enemies — both human and monstrous — to reach the man who was once his closest friend.

The story builds to an emotionally charged conclusion as Grave confronts Harry for the last time. Their final meeting is not only a clash of guns but of memories, regrets, and the lingering bond they once shared. Gungrave ultimately explores themes of loyalty, betrayal, and the tragic consequences of ambition, showing how the path from friendship to enmity can be paved with both good intentions and irreversible choices.
